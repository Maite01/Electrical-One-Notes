Circuit

Insulators

  • Materials that cannot pass electrical current easily

Uses of Insulators

  • Used to cover electric wires
  • Used to make casings of circuit breakers
  • Used in earth leakage devices and isolators

Types of Insulators

  • Rubber
  • Ceramic

Humidistat Controls

Function of a Control System

  • Ensures correct plant operation
  • Provides necessary safety protection
  • Ensures optimum efficiency

Thermostat Code Number

  • Used to specify a thermostat with specific parameters

Thermostat

  • A device that regulates temperature by controlling heating or cooling systems

Applications of Electrical Control

  • Relays
  • Contactors
  • Self-contained systems

Self-contained System

  • A system combining the controller and controlled device in one unit

Line Voltage

  • Normal electrical supply voltage
  • Voltage applied on the line

Low Voltage

  • Electrical wiring or devices using 30V or less
  • Devices usually function on 24V AC

Relay

  • A device consisting of a solenoid coil operating load-carrying contacts

Relay Contacts

  • Switches contacts when the coil is energized
  • Can be SPST or DPDT

Contacts

  • Contacts complete a circuit when the relay is energized
  • Examples: SPST, DPST, DPDT

Resistance Temperature Device (RTD)

  • Changes resistance with varying temperature
  • Has a positive temperature coefficient

Thermistor

  • Uses variation in resistance with changing temperature
  • Made from semiconductor material

Types of Thermistors

  • NTC — Resistance decreases as temperature increases
  • PTC — Resistance increases as temperature increases

Types of Control Devices

  • Operational control
  • Safety control
  • Efficiency control

Operational Control

  • Temperature controller uses a sensor mounted away from the control
  • Thermostat sensor is normally an integral part of the device

Characteristics of an Electromechanical Thermostat and an Electronic Controller

  • Both are single-stage ON/OFF devices

Safety Controls

  • Used in a control circuit as an interlock
  • Interrupts a control signal under specific conditions
  • Prevents equipment damage or loss of life

Bi-directional Electric Actuator

  • Two-position and floating type
  • Operates in both directions

Function of a Relay

  • Switches electrical controls using a solenoid coil

Single Phase Motor

  • Has two windings

  • Run winding

Single-Shaded Pole Motor

  • Has one winding

Electrical Load
